Teaching

Current & Upcoming

Quantitative Methods in Political Sociology (R Block Seminar)

University of Mannheim, FSS 2026 (upcoming)

Hands-on project-based seminar. Students develop proficiency in R programming and regression techniques, applying them to questions about intergroup relations, attitudes, and political identity. Culminates in an independent research paper.

Past Courses

Political Sociology: Conflict and Consensus - How Intergroup Relations Shape Society

University of Mannheim, HWS 2024 & HWS 2025 (BA-level seminar)

Covered social-psychological foundations of group formation, theories of intergroup relations applied to migration and ethnic diversity, and the role of digital media in social cohesion.

Methods in Political Sociology

University of Mannheim, FSS 2025

Applied R-based data analysis course. Students worked with ALLBUS survey data on intergroup attitudes, covering data management, regression, and visualization through a collaborative project.
