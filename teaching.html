Current & Upcoming

Übung: Quantitative Methods in Political Sociology (R Block Seminar)

University of Mannheim, FSS 2026 (upcoming)

In this seminar, students develop proficiency in R programming and quantitative methods through a hands-on project aimed at addressing questions in political sociology. The focus lies on modeling the links between intergroup relations, individual attitudes, and political identity using regression techniques. The project-based nature of the seminar enables students to understand and implement the data processing aspects of a research project. The acquired skills are applied to a self-chosen research question in a final paper.

Past Courses

Proseminar: Politische Soziologie — Konflikt und Konsens: Wie Intergruppenbeziehungen die Gesellschaft prägen

University of Mannheim, HWS 2024 & HWS 2025

BA-level seminar on intergroup relations in political sociology. The course begins with social-psychological foundations such as group formation, ingroup favoritism, and social cohesion. It then covers theories of intergroup relations, applying them to topics including migration, ethnic diversity, and other societal contexts. The final part examines how digital media affect intergroup relations and the opportunities and risks this poses for social cohesion in contemporary societies.

Übung: Methods in Political Sociology

University of Mannheim, FSS 2025

Applied methods course focused on quantitative data analysis using R. Students learned data management, statistical inference, linear and logistic regression, and data visualization through a collaborative project analyzing ALLBUS data on intergroup attitudes and contact behavior.
