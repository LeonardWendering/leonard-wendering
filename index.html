Leonard Wendering

Doctoral Researcher | CDSS, University of Mannheim | MZES

I am a doctoral researcher at the Mannheim Centre for European Social Research (MZES), University of Mannheim. In my research, I employ (quasi-)experimental methods to address questions regarding intergroup relations, focusing on minority experiences. I am also interested in the role of AI in social science research.

Current Projects

The Girl Next Door? Childhood Cross-Group Exposure and Inter-Ethnic Marriage

Linked full-count US Census data (1880-1910), tracking 400,000+ boys from childhood to adulthood to test whether having a neighbor of a different ethnicity increases interethnic marriage.

Ethnic neighborhoods in New York City (1880 Census) and specification curve of the effect of childhood cross-group exposure on interethnic marriage.

Lessons from an AI-Sprint: A Proposal for Measuring Human-AI Cooperation in Research

How can we understand and measure the dynamic between humans and AI when they collaborate on academic writing? We report results from a field experiment with early-career social scientists and propose a framework for measuring human-AI cooperation in research.
