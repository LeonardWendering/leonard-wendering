Leonard Wendering

PhD Student in Sociology | CDSS, University of Mannheim | MZES

I study intergroup relations with a focus on the minority perspective. My work combines causal inference techniques with register data to understand how interethnic exposure shapes long-term social outcomes. I am also interested in the role of AI in social science research.

My current projects examine childhood cross-group exposure and interethnic marriage using linked US Census data, the effects of terrorism on immigrants' cultural assimilation in Germany, and the impact of AI tools on academic writing. I have presented my work at international conferences including ISA 2025 (Rabat) and ECSR 2025 (Cologne).

Software

  • PhD Lunch Platform — Web application for automated monthly lunch group matching among PhD students (Python/Flask, PostgreSQL).
  • RAG Academic Search System — Retrieval-augmented generation system for semantic academic literature search (GROBID, FAISS).
