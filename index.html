Leonard Wendering

Doctoral Researcher | CDSS, University of Mannheim | MZES

I am a doctoral researcher at the Mannheim Centre for European Social Research (MZES), University of Mannheim. In my research, I employ (quasi-)experimental methods to address questions regarding intergroup relations, focusing on minority experiences. I am also interested in the role of AI in social science research.

Current Projects

Childhood Exposure to Ethnic Outgroups Predicts Interethnic Marriage

Using linked full-count US Census data (1880-1910) for over 400,000 men, we study how childhood exposure to ethnic outgroup neighbors affects interethnic marriage decisions decades later.

Crafting Cohesion: Interethnic Contact and Prejudice Reduction in Vocational Training

A field experiment studying whether interethnic contact during vocational training reduces prejudice among young adults in Germany.

Terrorism and Immigrants' Cultural Assimilation

Examining naming patterns before and after the 2016 Berlin Christmas Market attack to study how terrorism affects immigrants' cultural assimilation in Germany.

Lessons from an AI-Sprint: A Proposal for Measuring Human-AI Cooperation in Research

A field experiment pairing early-career researchers with and without AI tools, proposing a framework for measuring human-AI cooperation in academic writing.
